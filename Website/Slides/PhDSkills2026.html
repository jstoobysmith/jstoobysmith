Main skill:

Adaptability

But…
You can't practice adaptability directly.
What you can practice are foundational skills and working skills.

Foundational skills

Skills that pay off in any future.
  • Knowledge of the field and the role and capability of AI.
  • How to use AI effectively in your workflow.
  • How to be a valued member of the community.

Working skills

What you actually work on — depends on your view of the future.
  • Evaluating and building good AI models and workflows.
  • Human-in-the-loop solutions to mathematical and physics problems.
  • Formalization (more to come on this).

The path I found

Formalization and Lean

What is Lean?

An interactive theorem prover. Write definitions, theorems, and proofs and have guaranteed correctness, and mathematical consistency.
(Formalization = writing physics or maths into an ITP like Lean)
Trust
of the correctness
Assumptions
Made explicit
Reuse
Searchable & composable results

The recent driving forces

📚 Mathlib

  • Monolithic library of mathematics in Lean.
  • Large enough to work at a high level.
  • Open-source, community-driven, high standards for maintainability and reusability.

🤖 Artificial Intelligence

  • Tight feedback loop between AI and Lean.
  • Rapid improvement over recent months.
  • Open problems are starting to be solved this way.

Physlib

leanprover-community/Physlib
A community-driven project to digitalise results from physics into Lean.
Lean ⭐ 602 60+ contributors 7 maintainers
  • A monolithic library for physics results from standard physics (not just axiomisations).,
  • Research-level results, with carefully standardized definitions and theorems — a foundation for future formalization.
  • Community run (no bias to any academic or company) following open-source software workflows.
